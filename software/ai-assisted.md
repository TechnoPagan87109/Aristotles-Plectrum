Ai-assisted deliberation processing starts when Members begin rating the proposal. They can give the proposal an overall satisfaction rating, satisfaction rating for specific sections. The marked up proposals are collected and sorted by an ensemble AI and ML routines that analyze the proposals for AI-assisted processing begins when Members start evaluating a proposal.

Members may provide:

* an overall satisfaction rating,
* ratings for specific sections,
* comments, concerns, and suggested revisions.

These responses are collected and analyzed by a combination of AI and machine-learning systems.

The purpose of this analysis is not to make decisions, but to identify:

* areas of agreement,
* areas of disagreement,
* hidden assumptions,
* unintended consequences,
* and opportunities to improve overall satisfaction.

Based on this analysis, a revised proposal may be generated and distributed to the Members. At this point, analysis may generate unexpected information. Other forms of may tally ratings or count votes, but this system might tell why people are disagreeing. A deeper understanding of the problem(s) may arise.

Sometimes a proposal uncovers:

a genuine conflict of values,
a scarce resource,
a mutually exclusive goal.

In those cases satisfaction may:

plateau,
decline,
split into competing proposals.

The revised proposal is then evaluated through the same process. Satisfaction ratings and comments are collected, analyzed, and incorporated into future revisions. Iterations continue as long as they have support and produce further insight.

When a proposal reaches a sufficient level of support and stability, an acceptance vote becomes available.

The role of AI is to assist Members in understanding complex proposals and identifying opportunities for improvement. Final decisions remain the responsibility of the Members.
