# Commons Allocation Index (CAI)

## Overview

The Commons Allocation Index (CAI) is an internal accounting system used to coordinate access to shared resources within the Community.

It is not a currency.

It does not represent stored value, cannot be traded externally, and does not have a fixed monetary equivalent. Instead, it functions as a mechanism for managing constraints, priorities, and access to limited resources.

CAI exists to answer a practical question:

> Given finite shared resources, how should access be coordinated fairly and transparently?

---

## What CAI Is

CAI is:

- an internal allocation and accounting mechanism  
- a way to track resource usage and availability  
- a coordination tool for shared infrastructure  
- a system for balancing competing demands  

It is used to:

- schedule access to shared equipment  
- allocate limited materials or energy  
- manage production capacity  
- coordinate community-scale resources  

---

## What CAI Is Not

CAI is not:

- a currency  
- a store of value  
- a tradable asset  
- a wage or compensation system  

It cannot be:

- exchanged outside the Community  
- accumulated for speculative purposes  
- converted directly into money  

CAI does not represent ownership.  
It represents **access within constraints**.

---

## How CAI Functions

CAI exists primarily at the point of use.

In most cases, it has no explicit value until it is used to access a resource. This moment—when CAI is exchanged for actual goods, services, or capacity—is the point at which value becomes concrete.

This allows the system to:

- operate without continuous price assignment  
- avoid constant valuation of all activity  
- focus on allocation rather than accumulation  

---

## Relationship to Governance

CAI operates within the rules defined by Community governance.

- Governance determines how resources are prioritized  
- CAI enforces those priorities in practice  
- Members influence both through the deliberative process  

Changes to CAI rules (allocation methods, limits, priorities) are made through the same proposal and decision-making system as other Community rules.

---

## Relationship to the External Economy

The Community operates within a broader market economy.

CAI does not replace external currency systems. Instead:

- External trade uses conventional currency  
- Internal coordination uses CAI  

This separation allows the Community to:

- participate in existing economic systems  
- while organizing internal resources differently  

---

## Design Goals

CAI is designed to:

- prevent overuse of shared resources  
- reduce waste and inefficiency  
- maintain transparency in allocation  
- avoid concentration of control  
- support a near post-scarcity environment  

---

## Optional Extensions (Future Development)

CAI may later incorporate:

- responsibility weighting (linking access to stewardship or contribution)  
- dynamic allocation based on resource conditions  
- integration with gamification systems (without becoming one)  
- long-term resource planning models  

These features are not required for initial implementation.

---

## Summary

CAI is a coordination system, not an economic ideology.

It exists to ensure that shared resources are:
- used efficiently,
- allocated fairly,
- and managed transparently,

within a Community designed to operate beyond traditional scarcity-driven models.
