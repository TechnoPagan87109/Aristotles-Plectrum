# Community Operating System (COS) 

## Definition:
 
This is the (badly named) digital infrastructure that enables the Community to:
 - coordinate governance, 
 - resource management, 
 - communication, 
 - and daily operations.

## Why It Matters 

- Enables coordinated action at scale 
- Provides visibility into shared systems 
- Supports governance and decision-making 
- Reduces friction in daily operations 

## In This Project The COS allows Members to:

- participate in governance 
- monitor infrastructure systems 
- coordinate resource use and scheduling 
- communicate and share information 

It connects systems such as:
- energy (power grid) 
- water 
- waste 
- production equipment 

## Side Notes
- The COS should coordinate human intent, permissions, schedules, and resource priorities, while [ROS](/software/ros.md)-compatible systems execute physical or device-level tasks through standardized machine interfaces.
- The COS can be begun first and will aid in the rest of the Community

## Key Principles

- Transparency 
- Accessibility for Members 
- Integration across systems 
- Human-controlled decision-making 

## Key Questions 

- What level of automation is appropriate? 
- How is system reliability ensured? 
- How is access managed? 

## Related Concepts 

- Governance 
- AI-Assisted Systems 
- Ecovillage 
- drive towards Post-Scarcity. There are two 'forces' that will drive the Community towards Post-Scarcity. 

- First is about 'cost'. Studies have shown Open Source to be 30-90% cheaper. If you can save half the cost, you've essentially doubled your resources. 

- Second, there will always be tasks that are less agreeable than others. Those will be the tasks will finds some work-around. Then someone else will find an easy, more efficient way to do it, it becomes standard practice and it will spread to other Communities. One advance at a time that makes the process easier and push the Community towards Post-Scarcity. 

On the other hand, some people take pride and satisfaction in items they make by hand or with handtools, of wood or metal, that will last for generations. Others explore the beauty of textiles that add comfort to our living space. There are people out there, take away their garden, you've wounded their soul. We will decide, each one us, for ourselves, what tasks will be handing off to automation and it won't be for 'economics'. It will be the things we take pride in and add enjoyment and meaning to our lives.

### COS Layers

The Community Operating System is not a single application. It is a collection of systems operating at different levels.

### Governance Layer

Supports:

* proposal creation
* deliberation
* voting
* record keeping

### Communication Layer

Supports:

* Member-only social media
* messaging
* announcements
* knowledge sharing

### Knowledge Layer

Supports:

* educational materials
* reference materials
* Community documentation
* open-source resources

### Personal Assistance Layer

Supports:

* Private AI systems
* personal scheduling
* proposal analysis
* decision support

### Automation Layer

Supports:

* mobile autonomous devices
* stationary autonomous devices
* robotics
* monitoring systems

### Infrastructure Layer

Supports:

* energy systems
* water systems
* waste systems
* production systems
* resource monitoring

Each layer can evolve independently while remaining integrated with the rest of the Community.
