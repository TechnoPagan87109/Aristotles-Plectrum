# Conflict Resolution

## Status

Draft

## Purpose

This document describes how the Community addresses disputes, grievances, and conflicts between Members, families, the Community, and the Corporation.

## Overview

Conflict resolution is separate from ordinary decision-making.

Decision-making determines what the Community should do.

Conflict resolution addresses situations where Members or groups are in direct conflict, where trust has been damaged, or where a grievance must be heard.

The goal is not punishment first.

The goal is to restore workable relationships, clarify responsibilities, and prevent resentment from becoming permanent.

## Types of Conflict

The system may address conflicts between:

- Member and Member
- Member and Community
- Community and Member
- Member and Corporation
- Family and Family
- Family and Community
- Family and Member

## Principles

Conflict resolution should be:

- fair
- transparent where appropriate
- private where necessary
- accessible
- non-retaliatory
- focused on repair when possible
- capable of making binding decisions when required

## Process

A conflict may begin with a request for mediation or a formal grievance.

The process may include:

- identifying the issue
- identifying affected parties
- gathering relevant facts
- hearing each side
- clarifying harms, responsibilities, and desired outcomes
- proposing remedies
- recording agreements
- reviewing outcomes later

## AI Assistance

AI may help summarize claims, identify relevant rules, surface possible remedies, and reduce misunderstanding.

AI does not judge the conflict.

Humans remain responsible for interpretation, judgment, and final resolution.

## Anti-Retaliation

Members must be able to use the conflict resolution system without fear of retaliation, shunning, exclusion, or informal punishment.

A conflict resolution system that cannot protect people from social retaliation will fail.

## Relationship to Governance

Some conflicts may reveal problems in Community rules or systems.

When that happens, the conflict may produce a proposal for broader Community decision-making.

Conflict resolution handles the immediate dispute.

Decision-making handles changes to the system.
