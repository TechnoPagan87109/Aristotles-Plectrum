# CoHousing

## Status 🟢 Draft

## Definition

[CoHousing](cohousing.md) is an intentional community model in which residents live in private homes while sharing selected spaces, services, and infrastructure.

The Corporation supports a CoHousing-style living arrangement as part of its broader effort to emulate a near post-scarcity environment.

CoHousing provides a bridge between the individual household and the larger Ecovillage.

## Why It Matters
- Reduces duplication of infrastructure
- Encourages cooperation and social cohesion
- Improves efficiency of shared systems
- Supports participation in community processes

## In This Project
CoHousing provides the human-scale structure within the Ecovillage.

Members maintain private residences while sharing access to:

- kitchens (possibly)
- workshops
- production spaces
- social areas
- guest rooms

Shared spaces allow the Community to concentrate resources where they provide the greatest benefit while preserving privacy, autonomy, and personal living space.

CoHousing is intended to encourage cooperation without requiring collectivized living.

## Housing as a Special Resource

Not all Community resources are treated equally.

Housing occupies a unique position because it provides safety, stability, privacy, and long-term continuity.

For this reason, housing allocation may operate under rules that differ significantly from those used for other shared resources.

The Community seeks to provide a level of housing security that allows Members to participate in Community life without fear of arbitrary displacement.

## Key Principles
- Balance of private and shared space
- Voluntary participation in shared systems
- Social integration without enforced collectivism

## Key Questions
- What should be shared vs private?
- How are shared resources governed?
- How is conflict managed in shared spaces?
