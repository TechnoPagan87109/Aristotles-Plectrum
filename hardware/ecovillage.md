# Ecovillage

## Status Draft Complete

The ecovillage is the physical and ecological foundation of the community. It is designed as an integrated system where housing, food production, energy, water, sanitation, and manufacturing are treated as interconnected processes rather than separate domains.

A core principle is full resource accounting. Every input and output-materials, energy, water, and waste—is tracked. Outputs from one process are treated as inputs to another wherever possible, forming a continuously improving circular system. This approach
emphasizes efficiency, transparency, and long-term sustainability rather than isolated optimization.
Housing plays a central role in this system and serves as a primary area of experimentation. Structures are designed to be durable, maintainable, and materially efficient. Techniques such as compressed earth construction are used not as novelty, but as practical alternatives to conventional methods where appropriate.
Rather than rejecting existing building standards, the community treats them as a baseline. New approaches are tested, instrumented, and evaluated using embedded sensors and real-time data. This allows the community to compare performance—structural, thermal, environmental, and economic—against conventional construction and refine designs over time.
Housing is not treated as a standalone product. It is integrated with:

food production (e.g., attached or nearby growing systems),

energy generation and storage,

water collection and treatment,

and waste processing.

The goal is to reduce external dependencies while improving resilience and quality of life. By bringing production closer to consumption and making system performance visible, the ecovillage becomes a continuously evolving, data-informed environment.
This experimental approach extends beyond housing to the entire physical system of the community. The ecovillage functions as a testbed for practical, scalable improvements in how communities manage resources, build infrastructure, and sustain themselves over time.
